Pipelining

A single-cycle processor, executing a program with 1000 instructions, has an operating frequency of 100 MHz. The processor was pipelined, which caused its CPI to increase by 20%.
Assume that we were able to distribute the delay among the pipeline stages perfectly, and we are using the new maximum usable frequency.
If the new execution time is 2 x $10^{-6}s$, into how many stages was the processor pipelined?
Ignore the fill-in time (time at the beginning of program execution when pipeline is being filled) and drain time (time at the end of the program when the pipelined is being drained).

Parallelism

I proposed 3 improvements for my processor, only two which can be implemented.
(a) Improvement A halved the execution time of 20% of instructions.
(b) Improvement B quartered the execution time of 50% of instructions not affected by Improvement A.
(c) Improvement C halved the execution of 80% of instructions not affected by Improvements A & B.
Which two improvements combined yields the most speedup? Show all possible speedups.
